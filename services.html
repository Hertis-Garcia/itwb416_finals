Student Services

Purpose: Provide support and resources to students. Below are services, policies, and contact pointers to help students navigate academic advising, enrollment, internships, student organizations, laboratory use, and financial assistance.

Academic Advising

▾

Academic advising ensures all students are guided by their professors in each respective course. Faculty members allot consultation hours outside the requisite classroom lectures to make themselves available to address other student concerns. However, schedules may vary so students should personally coordinate and confirm consultation times with their professors.

Enrollment & Registration Guidelines

▾

Enrollment Policy

A student is considered officially enrolled after making full or initial payment of tuition and other fees. A registration confirmation slip printed from the online enrollment facility (OnE) may be used for payment in accredited payment centers and banks or at the University cashiers. Once the University officially acknowledges payment ("posting") in OnE, the student may print the Certificate of Registration (COR) — Transaction 1. Modification and registration of enrolled subjects will mean Transaction 2 or 3.

  • A student may only modify registration online if Transaction 1 is complete (payment posted in OnE).
  • Students are allowed a maximum of three transactions. A fourth transaction may be requested case-by-case via an Enrollment Transaction Request Form and is subject to University Registrar approval and the corresponding fee.
  • Students with unpaid balances or old accounts are not allowed to enroll until cleared by the Accounting Office.
  • Foreign students will be placed on hold and will only be cleared upon presentation of certificate of eligibility to enroll.

For more information, visit the Registrar's enrollment page: dlsud.edu.ph/registrar/enrollment.htm

Internship / On-the-Job Training (OJT) Information

▾

The Internship/On-the-Job Training (OJT) is a mandatory component designed to provide students with hands-on, real-world experience in the IT industry, bridging the gap between classroom theory and professional practice. Detailed procedures and placements are discussed during the student's 4th year; students should monitor program announcements and consult their program council (CSPC/ITPC) or OJT coordinator for timelines and requirements.

Laboratory Policies

▾

These rules are established to ensure efficient operation and maintenance of computer laboratory resources for academic purposes.

  1. Usage Priority and Conduct
    • Academic Priority: laboratory computers are primarily for coursework, projects, and research. Academic users have priority.
    • Silence and Order: observe silence; set phones to silent; avoid disruptive activity.
    • No Loitering: labs are for productive use; no sleeping or excessive socializing.
  2. Equipment and Facility Care
    • Food & Drink: eating, drinking, and chewing gum are strictly prohibited.
    • No Tampering: do not move, unplug, dismantle, or tamper with hardware; report malfunctions immediately.
    • Cleanliness: keep workstations tidy and dispose of trash properly.
  3. Network and Ethical Use
    • Academic Use Only: internet access is for educational and research activities.
    • Prohibited Content: accessing or distributing sexually explicit, offensive, or malicious material is prohibited and may result in disciplinary action.
    • Respect Intellectual Property: do not copy or distribute copyrighted software or illegal files.
    • Network Abuse: playing games, running network-intensive apps for non-academic use, or unauthorized scanning/hacking is forbidden.
    • Log Off: always log off and close applications before leaving a workstation.

Computer Lab Rules & Booking

Booking procedures and lab schedules are typically posted in the department or managed via the Laboratory Assistant. Check bulletin boards or contact your program office for the current lab booking process and times.
